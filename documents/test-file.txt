Job Title: Entry-level Accountant

Job Summary:
The entry-level Accountant will be responsible for assisting with various accounting tasks within the organization. This role primarily involves maintaining financial records, preparing financial reports, and supporting the overall financial operations. The ideal candidate should possess strong quantitative skills, attention to detail, and a basic understanding of accounting principles.

Duties and Responsibilities:
- Assist with day-to-day accounting tasks, including data entry, accounts receivable, accounts payable, and general ledger maintenance.
- Reconcile financial discrepancies by collecting and analyzing account information.
- Prepare financial reports by collecting, analyzing, and summarizing account information and trends.
- Assist in accurately calculating payroll and monitoring employee expense claims.
- Support in the preparation of monthly, quarterly, and annual financial statements.
- Ensure financial records are properly maintained and comply with relevant regulations.
- Help with the preparation and filing of tax forms and other compliance-related documents.
- Collaborate with other teams within the organization to gather and validate financial data.
- Ensure confidentiality and security of financial transactions and information.

Qualifications:
- Bachelor's degree in Accounting, Finance, or related field.
- Strong understanding of basic accounting principles and financial concepts.
- Proficiency in MS Office Suite, especially Excel, and accounting software.
- Excellent numerical and analytical skills.
- Attention to detail and ability to work with accuracy.
- Strong organization and time management skills, with the ability to meet deadlines.
- Ability to work both independently and collaboratively within a team environment.
- Strong written and verbal communication skills.
- Knowledge of relevant regulations and compliance requirements is a plus.

Note: This job description is intended to provide a general overview of the position and may vary depending on the specific needs of the organization.
